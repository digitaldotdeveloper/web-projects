Where Batroun meets the sea

Where the land does not end, but opens.

The Golden Sky Hotel was imagined from the meeting point of stone and sea, heritage and modernity, stillness and light. Built above the Phoenician coast, the hotel follows the natural rhythm of the cliffs — terraces step toward the water, arches frame the horizon, pools reflect the sky. Every view returns to the Mediterranean.

CHAPTER II · THE ARRIVAL
the arrival — the arrival — the arrival —
Stone, shadow, and light

A grand entrance into quiet luxury.

The arrival begins with stone, shadow, and light. A private drive leads through landscaped gardens toward the hotel's monumental entrance, where arches, lanterns, and the golden wordmark welcome guests into a world of calm Mediterranean elegance. Luxury begins before the door opens.

A five-star arrival

Composed, never loud — the warm stone, the valet court, the scent of the sea, the soft movement of palms, the glow of sunset on the façade.

Every detail of the arrival is designed to feel deliberate. The drive curves to soften your approach. The colonnade frames the horizon. The first concierge greets you by name.

Pools, cabanas, and golden-hour stillness

Time becomes softer here.

At the heart of the hotel, infinity pools stretch toward the horizon. The water reflects the sky, the stone, and the shifting colors of the Mediterranean day. Around the pool terraces, modern cabanas, soft loungers, and landscaped gardens create shaded spaces for slow afternoons before sunset.

A sanctuary of stone and water

Here, wellness is not performance. It is stillness.

The spa is inspired by Mediterranean bathing rituals, Lebanese stone, and the quiet power of water. Warm arches, soft lighting, indoor pools, private treatment spaces, and calm textures create a place for restoration.

Mediterranean under the Golden Sky

Breakfast begins with light over the water. Evenings unfold beneath limestone arches.

Dining at The Golden Sky Hotel celebrates the flavors and rituals of the Lebanese coast. Fresh seafood, olive oil, herbs, seasonal produce, warm bread, mezze, and Mediterranean simplicity, served in refined sea-facing settings.

Four kitchens, one coastline

The cuisine is elegant, generous, and connected to the place it comes from.
