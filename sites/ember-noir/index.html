I. Manifesto

A whisper of midnight, bound in ember.

Ember Noir is not a cigar — it is a slow argument with time itself. Leaves chosen in the gloom before sunrise. Rolled by four hands that have known the same bench for forty years. Sealed inside cedar darkened with linseed and bone-black.

Each batch waits thirteen winters in our cellar beneath the river. What you finally light is the last argument we lost — and the first one you will win.

— Esteban N. Vargas Maestro Tabacalero

III. The Ritual

How to begin an evening.

  1. 01

    Cut clean

    A single, decisive snip — a sixteenth of an inch above the line. The cigar should never be wounded twice.

  2. 02

    Toast the foot

    Hold the flame a finger's width below. Rotate. Wait for the ember to remember its own light.

  3. 03

    Draw the first hour

    No rush. No phone. The first hour of an Ember Noir is a small private country, and you are its only citizen.
